Raspberry Bars



DESCRIPTION

Tart raspberry filling is swirled into a low-fat cream filling in these beautiful bars.
They're a festive treat for a summer picnic or party.


INGREDIENTS


Crust

3/4 cup white whole-wheat flour

1/2 cup chopped pecans

2 tablespoons granulated sugar

1/2 teaspoon salt

3 tablespoons cold butter, cut into small pieces

2 tablespoons ice water

1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract 		Raspberry Filling

2 teaspoons unflavored gelatin

2 tablespoons water

3 cups fresh raspberries, divided

1/2 cup granulated sugar

4 tablespoons nonfat cream cheese, softened

2 tablespoons low-fat milk

1 tablespoon confectioners’ sugar

PREPARATION


  1. To prepare crust:
    Preheat oven to 400°F. Coat an 8-inch-square baking pan with cooking spray.

  2. Place flour, pecans, 2 tablespoons sugar and salt in a food processor process
    until the nuts are finely ground.

  3. Add butter one piece at a time, pulsing once or twice after each addition, until incorporated.
    Add ice water and vanilla and pulse just until the dough starts to come together.

  4. Transfer to the prepared pan. Press evenly and firmly into the pan to form a bottom crust.

  5. Bake the crust until it looks set, but not browned, about 15 minutes. Let cool on a wire rack.

  6. To prepare raspberry filling:
    Sprinkle gelatin over 2 tablespoons water in a small bowl; let stand,
    stirring once or twice, while you prepare the rest of the filling.

  7. Reserve 16 raspberries. Puree the remaining raspberries in a food processor until smooth.
    Transfer to a medium saucepan and stir in 1/2 cup sugar.

  8. Cook over medium heat until bubbling. Stir in the gelatin mixture and cook,
    stirring, until the gelatin is melted, about 1 minute.

  9. Fill a large bowl with ice water. Pour the raspberry mixture into a medium bowl
    and set it in the bowl of ice water. Refrigerate, stirring occasionally with a rubber spatula,
    until the mixture thickens to the consistency of loose jam and is beginning
    to set around the edges, about 30 minutes.

  10. Meanwhile, beat cream cheese, milk and confectioners' sugar in a medium bowl
    with an electric mixer until smooth.

  11. Spread the thickened raspberry filling evenly over the crust.
    Dollop the cream cheese mixture over the filling.

  12. Draw the tip of a sharp knife or skewer through the two fillings to create a swirled effect.
    Nestle the reserved berries into the filling, evenly spacing them so each bar will be topped
    with a berry when cut.

  13. Refrigerate until the bars are completely set, about 3 hours.

  14. Cut into 16 bars, one raspberry per bar.


TIPS

Cover and refrigerate for up to 1 day.
